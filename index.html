Section 02 / Problem & Solution

From Manual Matching to a Connected Procure-to-Pay Layer

What we heard from your team on the May 18 and June 18 calls, and how Reeco closes the gap without replacing the systems you already run on.

01
The Problem

Manual matching at the property level, slow coding, no forecast at the point of purchase.

Avion runs purchasing through Food Buy's One Source punch-out model, with invoice processing and PO matching handled manually at the property level and final approval sitting with AP. Coding and three-way matching are slow, and forecast visibility doesn't reach the buyer at the moment of purchase.

The Answer

Reeco sits on top of your stack and automates the work between systems.

Reeco sits on top of M3, Profit Sword, and Food Buy and automates the work between them. Invoices are captured, AI-coded, three-way matched, and pushed to M3 automatically. Buyers see real-time Profit Sword forecast impact at the moment they order, so spend stays inside budget before it happens, not after.

Section 03 / The Platform

One Connected Workflow, Built for Hotels

Four modules, adopted in the combination each property needs.

Purchasing & Receiving

Every Food Buy-approved vendor in one marketplace, not punch-out. Build a cross-vendor cart and check out once; the system learns each user's GL coding to automate it over time. Mobile receiving with three-way match.

AP Automation

Photo or upload an invoice; AI codes multiple GL lines and completes the three-way match into M3. Reeco advocates directly with vendors on discrepancies, credits, and short-ships.

Inventory Management

Add-On

Mobile storeroom counts and par-to-cart reordering across multiple storerooms, built for complex F&B operations such as The Windsor Court.

Culinary Management

Add-On

Menu and recipe costing with POS integration (e.g., Micros) to track food cost and profitability by recipe.

The Operator Experience

A clean mobile app for invoice capture, inventory counts, and approvals, plus a corporate dashboard giving regional approvers a consolidated view across all properties. Avion operations leadership called out the mobile experience on the June 18 demo.

Section 06 / Implementation

Your Path to Go-Live

A four-wave, 120-day rollout across all 40 properties. Each wave kicks off 30 days after the last and builds on it, so you're never re-learning the process. Built from your team's May 18, June 18, and July 14 calls.

01
Day 0
Wave 1 · Pilot

Five hotels first, then apply it portfolio-wide.

Select five properties to align our teams on Avion's process. Prework and data gathering begin across the full portfolio, broadliner suppliers are built into the platform, and we align on operational success criteria before scaling what works.

02
Day 30
Wave 2 · AP + Select-Service Purchasing

AP live in one week, portfolio-wide.

Reeco pulls everything from M3, so AP automation goes live across the full portfolio within a week and training wraps in week 1. Select-service purchasing implementation and additional supplier build-out run in parallel.

03
Day 60
Wave 3 · Full-Service Purchasing & Inventory

Full-service purchasing goes live; Inventory onboards where needed.

Full-service purchasing implementation follows the same playbook as Wave 2, supplier catalogs are finalized, and any hotels adding the Inventory module are onboarded alongside it.

04
Day 90
Wave 4 · Culinary

Every hotel live on every module.

The Culinary module is implemented wherever applicable, completing the rollout across all 40 properties by Day 120.

Every wave runs the same cadence: Collect & Connect in the first two weeks (ideally handled as prework before Day 1), Setup to Live by day 30, then Ongoing Support & Improvement through Day 120 as adoption deepens.

Section 08 / FAQ

Questions, Answered Directly

If something's missing, email Zander and we'll add it before the proposal review.

How fast can we go live?
Implementation is product-guided and self-serve. AP automation can be live within about one week with minimal property effort; the purchasing marketplace and self-paced Academy training follow over roughly a month on the same foundation. There's no property-by-property hand-holding. Champions are onboarded together and properties go live as they complete training. Reeco structures the contract to avoid double-paying during the transition off One Source.
How does the M3 integration hold up?
Reeco runs a real-time, two-way daily sync with M3, its most proven integration, with PO spend and GL/budget data syncing automatically every day. The true failure rate runs under 1%. Most of what looks like a sync failure is the system working as intended, correctly blocking duplicate invoices and entries posted to closed accounting periods.
Is the platform secure and audit-ready?
Yes. Reeco is SOC 2 Type II. Additional audit documentation, including vendor setup controls and W-9 handling, is provided for your ownership groups' auditors.
How are accruals and prepaid bulk purchases handled?
Reeco is confirming the exact treatment for prepaid inventory allocated across months and will report back. This is an open item from the June 18 demo.
How do duplicate invoices get caught?
Reeco automatically detects duplicate invoices and blocks them from posting to M3 before they ever double-count. This is built into the M3 sync and is the single largest category of entries the system intentionally stops.
How has catalog completeness improved, and how much of the vendor catalog is maintained in the platform?

Reeco supports over 5,000 suppliers with fully maintained catalogs. For a portfolio like Avion’s, the setup conversation is about limiting the marketplace to your approved suppliers, not filling catalog gaps.

What has improved in product search, purchasing efficiency, and user experience?

Search now shows comparable items side by side across your suppliers, so buyers make the right call in one view. Teams find and source what they need quickly while corporate keeps full visibility and controls.

Does Reeco provide detailed product descriptions, specifications, and images?

Yes. Reeco maintains a standardized data layer on top of the major suppliers, so products carry consistent descriptions, specifications, and images across the marketplace.

Are PO and invoice approval workflows fully configurable to our purchasing policies and approval hierarchies?

Yes, fully configurable at both the PO and invoice level. Role based templates are rolling out so a new GM can run a different approval flow than a seasoned one.

Can users include notes and special instructions for vendors directly on purchase orders?

Yes. Notes and special instructions carry through to the supplier with the order, and Reeco’s fulfillment team makes sure instructions are honored with suppliers whose systems do not accept them electronically.

How frequently are vendor prices updated, and how is contract pricing incorporated?

Broadline supplier pricing refreshes every time your team logs in, and saved order lists update nightly. Offline suppliers update automatically from invoices and price files. Contract and promotional pricing is maintained directly with the GPOs.

How does Reeco recommend lower cost equivalent products while maintaining quality?

Side by side comparisons at search, an AI suggested cart built from your invoices, inventory, and recipes, and a personalized store that surfaces GPO approved and brand standard items first, so quality stays intact.

Can Reeco demonstrate measurable procurement savings beyond administrative efficiencies?

Yes. One management company brought food and beverage cost from 40% down to 25% using these tools. On the AP side, teams save 7 to 10 minutes per invoice, roughly 14 hours of finance time per property each month.

What shipped in the last 12 months, and what is on the roadmap?

Self guided onboarding, role based approval standards, the ProfitSword integration, and a corporate checkbook giving budget and forecast views across every property. Ahead: personalized AI stores per property and expanded GPO partnerships.

How is AI evolving beyond AP to improve purchasing, inventory, and procurement performance?

Reeco’s agents build a personalized store for each property from your live supplier accounts, the suggested cart learns from your invoices, inventory, and recipes, and every purchase is checked against your ProfitSword forecast at the point of order.
