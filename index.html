Section 02  /  Problem & Solution

From Manual Matching to a Connected Procure-to-Pay Layer

What we heard from your team on the May 18 and June 18 calls — and how Reeco closes the gap without replacing the systems you already run on.

The Problem

Coding and three-way matching are slow, and forecast visibility doesn't reach the buyer.

Avion runs purchasing through Food Buy's One Source punch-out model, with invoice processing and PO matching handled manually at the property level and final approval sitting with AP. Coding and three-way matching are slow, and forecast visibility doesn't reach the buyer at the moment of purchase.

The Answer

Reeco sits on top of your stack and automates the work between systems.

Reeco sits on top of M3, Profit Sword, and Food Buy and automates the work between them. Invoices are captured, AI-coded, three-way matched, and pushed to M3 automatically. Buyers see real-time Profit Sword forecast impact at the moment they order, so spend stays inside budget before it happens, not after.

Section 03  /  The Platform

One Connected Workflow, Built for Hotels

Four modules, adopted in the combination each property needs.

Purchasing & Receiving

Every Food Buy-approved vendor in one marketplace — not punch-out. Build a cross-vendor cart and check out once; the system learns each user's GL coding to automate it over time. Mobile receiving with three-way match.

AP Automation

Photo or upload an invoice; AI codes multiple GL lines and completes the three-way match into M3. Reeco advocates directly with vendors on discrepancies, credits, and short-ships.

Inventory Management

Add-On

Mobile storeroom counts and par-to-cart reordering across multiple storerooms — built for complex F&B operations such as The Windsor Court.

Culinary Management

Add-On

Menu and recipe costing with POS integration (e.g., Micros) to track food cost and profitability by recipe.

The Operator Experience

A clean mobile app for invoice capture, inventory counts, and approvals, plus a corporate dashboard giving regional approvers a consolidated view across all properties. Avion operations leadership called out the mobile experience on the June 18 demo.

Section 06  /  Implementation

Your Path to Go-Live

A guided, product-led rollout across all 40 properties — most of it self-serve, with Reeco support where you want it. AP automation is live in about a week; the full purchasing marketplace follows over roughly a month on the same foundation — and your contract is structured so you're never double-paying during the transition off One Source.

01
Week 1
Kickoff

Two short calls, then the platform leads.

A management kickoff and a property-champion kickoff confirm your property list (17 full-service, 23 select-service), approval hierarchy, GL structure, and integration credentials for M3, Profit Sword, and Food Buy (One Source). No lengthy per-property discovery calls — champions are onboarded together.

02
~Week 1
Guided Setup & AP Go-Live

An automated wizard connects M3 — AP live in about a week.

Reeco's setup wizard connects your real-time M3 API and Profit Sword feed and pulls in your vendors. AP automation goes live first — invoices captured, AI-coded, and three-way matched into M3 with minimal property effort.

03
Weeks 2–4
Purchasing Marketplace, Self-Connected

Your Food Buy-approved catalog, built in the background.

Your Food Buy (One Source)-approved suppliers are loaded into one marketplace; your team self-connects suppliers with a few clicks, and Reeco builds each store automatically. GL coding, receiving with three-way match, and the corporate approval dashboard for regional approvers are configured along the way.

04
Weeks 3–4
Academy Training & Go-Live

Self-paced training that ends in a real action.

Property teams complete the self-paced Reeco Academy — AP first, then Purchasing — on their own schedule. Training finishes when a user takes the real action (first invoice synced, first order placed), and properties go live as champions complete it. No hands-on per-property visits required.

05
Ongoing
Adoption & Add-Ons

Management-driven adoption, with Reeco on standby.

An Adoption Dashboard tracks usage per property with automated nudges, and a dedicated CSM runs a 30/60/90 success plan. Inventory and Culinary modules layer onto complex F&B properties such as The Windsor Court when ready. Prefer a fully hands-on rollout? Reeco Plus white-glove onboarding is available as a paid upgrade.

Implementation is product-guided and self-serve, with Reeco support throughout — time-to-value is fast because the M3 integration is a live, real-time API and your team is guided through setup and training in the platform.

Section 08  /  FAQ

Questions, Answered Directly

If something's missing, email Zander and we'll add it before the proposal review.

How fast can we go live?
Implementation is product-guided and self-serve. AP automation can be live within about one week with minimal property effort; the purchasing marketplace and self-paced Academy training follow over roughly a month on the same foundation. There's no property-by-property hand-holding — champions are onboarded together and properties go live as they complete training. Reeco structures the contract to avoid double-paying during the transition off One Source.
How does the M3 integration hold up?
Reeco runs a real-time, two-way daily sync with M3 — its most proven integration, with PO spend and GL/budget data syncing automatically every day. Reeco will share M3 integration error-rate data as part of diligence.
Is the platform secure / audit-ready?
Yes — Reeco is SOC 2 Type II. Additional audit documentation (vendor setup controls, W-9 handling) will be provided for your ownership groups' auditors.
How are accruals and prepaid bulk purchases handled?
Reeco is confirming the exact treatment for prepaid inventory allocated across months and will report back (open item from the June 18 demo).
How do duplicate invoices get caught?
Reeco will document its duplicate-invoice detection methodology and the M3 error-handling flow as part of the follow-up.
User administration / SSO?
User add/remove is handled by an admin today; SSO is not currently in place. Approval workflows are configurable per property.
